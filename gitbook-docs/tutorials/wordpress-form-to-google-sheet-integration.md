# WordPress Form to Google Sheet Integration

## Streamline Data Collection Efforts with a Super Forms to Google Sheets Integration via Zapier

By integrating Super Forms with Google Sheets through Zapier, you can automate your data collection process, saving time and resources.

This integration allows you to transfer form submissions from Super Forms directly into Google Sheets, eliminating the need for manual data entry. This not only ensures accuracy but also enhances efficiency, allowing you to focus on analysis rather than tedious tasks.

Whether you're managing contact forms, surveys, or lead generation forms on your WordPress site, this integration helps you centralize and organize your data in one place. By utilizing Google Sheets for your form submissions, you can easily analyze and manipulate data to gain insights and make informed decisions.

### Step-by-Step Guide on Integrating Super Forms with Google Sheets via Zapier

The below guide will help you integrate Super Forms with Google Sheets using Zapier in a few easy steps:

1. **Set Up Zapier:**
   * Create a Zapier Account: Sign up at [Zapier.com](https://zapier.com) if you don’t already have an account.
   * Click the Super Forms invitation link: [https://zapier.com/developer/public-invite/95800/cb161c44ef2edf4eb65fe007c21e34a1/](https://zapier.com/developer/public-invite/95800/cb161c44ef2edf4eb65fe007c21e34a1/) and click "Accept Invite & Build a Zap".
2. **Creating your Zap:**
   * Click "+ Create" and choose "Zap" to create a new Zap.
   * As the Trigger search for "Super Forms".
   * Set the "Trigger event" to "New Form Submission (triggers when the form is submitted).
3. **Setting up Google Sheets connection:**
   * If you haven't already, create a new Google Sheet to be used for this Zap (or skip this step if you already have one)
   * In Zapier dashboard, as the "Action" choose "Google Sheets".
   * Now set the "Action event" to "Update Spreadsheet Row".
   * Now connect your Google Account with Zapier.
   * Choose your "Spreadsheet" and desired "Worksheet" and the "Row ID" to add the new data to, this typically should be the last Row in the spreadsheet
4. **Mapping form fields with Google Sheet document:**
   * Now map your form fields with the ones in the Google sheet, you can do so by clicking the "+" icon next to each column.
   * If you are asked to "Complete this step", click the button and copy the Webhook URL. You will need to paste this on your form by editing it and navigating to "Form Settings > Zapier".
   * Enable zapier for your form and make sure to enter the Webhook URL provided by Zapier. The URL should look something like this:\
     `https://hooks.zapier.com/hooks/catch/2103251/2d3e581/`
   * Save your form, and submit your form as a test, so that Zapier can read what data was sent by Super Forms.
5. **Test and Activate Your Zap:**
   * Check your Google Sheet to verify that the data is being captured correctly.
   * Once the test is successful, activate your Zap to automate the process fully.
